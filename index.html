History of Potlucks

Potlucks began in the early 1920's as a way to fight prohibition. Guests attending a party would be seen carrying large crock posts and casserole dishes under the guise of supplying food and treats for a wholesome night in. In reality, they had baked bottles of bootleg gin and other sundries into their dishes. Once inside, guests would eat around the booze until the bottles were revealed. Then they would drink to their genius. The term “pot luck” refers to the “luck” of finding high proof alcohol in such an unassuming “pot”, as in “This potato salad has brandy inside! My pot luck has turned!” This trend caught on with non-drinkers, who, unaware of the ruse, began throwing their own potluck parties sans alcohol. Today potlucks may or may not include alcohol, but thanks to the 21st amendment, the drinks no longer need to be hidden.
