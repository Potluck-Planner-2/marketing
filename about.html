Victoria

Backend Developer

Victoria is a potluck black belt. Raised in a community of casserole culture with a last name that always fell in the "dessert" category, her breakout dish was her mother's famous peanut butter bars. Now living on her own as an adult with access to an instant pot, she prefers to bring dishes such as kalua pork or carnitas. There have been no accusations of poisoned dishes but she would like to be ahead of the curve by vigorously denying any poison present in any of her contributions.

Barbara

Frontend Developer

Barbara spent her first 5 years out of college as a preschool teacher, and is therefore extremely well-versed in potlucks. Widely appreciated by parents, vegetarians, and people on diets, her typical potluck contribution is a veggie tray and some ranch dressing. Boring? Perhaps. An easy cop-out? Absolutely.

Patrick

Frontend Developer

Patrick and Potlucks. I know what your thinking, this isn't the name of some cool underground indie rock group. Or the name of your chauffer driving a stretched out lambo with champaign and nachocheese dip in the back seat. The P and P is the star at every Potluck party. He is particularly loved for his homemade steak and pizza hotpockets.

Lisa

Backend Developer

Lisa is a web-dev padawan, longboarder, bread baker, reluctant runner, ventriloquist whistler, she never forgets to floss, and is a friend to all cats. Her go-to potluck dish is orange jello, covered in marshmallows, topped with a thick layer of freshly whipped cream and a light sprinkling of chopped up jelly beans. She insists that it is a side dish and NOT a dessert.
